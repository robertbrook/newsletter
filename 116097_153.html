... brought up with "a traditional Islamic education", fought in the British Army in India, emigrated to Ireland, learned English, "eloped with an Anglo-Irish gentlewoman, Jane Daly", converted to Anglicanism, wrote "the first book ever written and published in English by an Indian", introduced "Indian therapeutic whole body massage", opened "the Hindostanee Coffee House" and served curries until going bust. Then, he moved to Brighton and started up all over again; his own bath house gained a "royal warrant as shampooing surgeon to George IV and William IV" and he opened two London branches. He died, aged 102, of "natural decay".
