http://onethingwell.org
One thing I've always liked is the idea of "one thing well". It's a Unix thing, I think. But there's two ends to that telescope: there are little applications that do that one thing well - there are lots of those. Then there are those applications which are more substantial, with a strong primary function. Vim and emacs would be examples. But these are pretty rare now - most developers can't resist either feature creep, or even platform creep. We should have a Windows version! We should have an Android version! No, you shouldn't. Stick to the knitting.
