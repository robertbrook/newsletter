I was lent a copy of this book - on Mother Teresa - recently, by someone I think has some vestigial religious faith. I don't. That doesn't mean I rejected the book, but the whole thing is just stuff I don't have in my life at all. I don't refuse the faith of others - but many people do seem to expect it in everyone. Some of us aren't searching for meaning, or indeed anything. Some of us don't feel absence, or loss, of ... whatever it is people with faith actually hold to, or feel. The most interesting thing, for me, is that what causes seemingly terrible pain in some - the crisis of faith - is everyday life for others, with no pain felt.
