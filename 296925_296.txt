After my recent fretting, Lloyd Davis notes that "The Lord Peter Wimsey theme is from 'When Day is Done' by the Paul Whiteman Orchestra - that muted cornet is played by Henry Busse who lived the sort of colourful life you and I can only dream of ..."

http://victor.library.ucsb.edu/index.php/matrix/detail/800013489/CVE-39227-When_day_is_done (http://victor.library.ucsb.edu/index.php/matrix/detail/800013489/CVE-39227-When_day_is_done )
... here's a record of that record, featuring "5 violins, bass, 4 saxophones, 3 cornets, 3 trombones, tuba, banjo, 2 pianos, and traps". Traps?

... and the life of Busse certainly was colourful: he emigrated from Germany to the United States in the middle of the First World War; a year later had a job as a trumpeter; a year after that joined an orchestra and started writing hit tunes; worked as an extra in the 'Keystone Cops'; earned $350 when fellow band member Bing Crosby was on $150 a week; worked for Al Capone; "partied one night with a woman at the Hotsy Totsy Club and woke up married" - and really did die while playing at an undertakers' convention, aged 60.
